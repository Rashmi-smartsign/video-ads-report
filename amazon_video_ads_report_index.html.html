Executive Summary

What This Period Tells Us

P1 delivered +30.2% SB Ad Sales ($72.2K) and a +27.3% total channel lift ($699K), driven primarily by re-enabling 178 video campaigns across 149 ASINs that had zero activity in P2. Sessions grew +19.4% while sales grew +27.3% — meaning Video is generating higher-intent shoppers who convert better, not just more traffic. The NTB rate held firm at 75.3%, confirming these campaigns are acquiring new customers rather than recycling existing demand. The 178 newly re-enabled campaigns alone posted 85.5% NTB purchase rate, making the incremental customer acquisition case compelling. The ROAS dip from 3.36x to 3.29x is a healthy, expected cost of scaling — not a warning signal.

NTB Rate Held While Sales Scaled +30%

NTB Sales grew $41.4K → $54.4K (+31.4%) while the NTB rate barely moved (74.6% → 75.3%). Scaling normally dilutes NTB — the fact it held signals Video is reaching genuinely fresh audiences, not saturated ones.

85.5% NTB Rate in 178 Newly Re-Enabled Campaigns

260 of 304 purchases came from first-time brand buyers — 10pp above the blended portfolio rate. At $28.77 NTB AOV and $15.36 cost-per-acquisition, this is highly efficient new customer growth.

Why NTB Matters: 260 NTB purchases at $15.36 CAC across 178 newly re-enabled campaigns — for $25–50+ products, this LTV:CAC ratio is strong. NTB rate above 70% must be protected as a core Video Ads KPI.
